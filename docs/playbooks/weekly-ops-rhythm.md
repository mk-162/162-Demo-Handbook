# Weekly Operations Rhythm

**Purpose:** Maintain operational excellence through consistent weekly cadence  
**Owner:** [CUSTOMIZE: Operations Manager / Director]  
**Time commitment:** ~4 hours/week for leadership, distributed across team

---

## Overview

High-performing ecommerce operations run on rhythm, not firefighting. This playbook establishes the weekly operating cadence for a B2B ecommerce business.

**Core principle:** Problems surface in data before they surface in complaints.

---

## Weekly Calendar

### Monday: Planning & Priorities

| Time | Activity | Owner | Duration |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 | Week-ahead planning | Ops Lead | 30 min |
| 09:30 | Outstanding issues review | Team | 15 min |
| 10:00 | Stock position check | Buying | 30 min |
| 11:00 | Capacity planning | Warehouse | 30 min |

**Monday planning checklist:**
- [ ] Review last week's metrics vs targets
- [ ] Identify this week's priorities (max 3)
- [ ] Flag resource constraints
- [ ] Confirm key deadlines
- [ ] Update team on any changes

### Tuesday: Customer & Quality Focus

| Time | Activity | Owner | Duration |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 | Customer complaint review | CS Lead | 30 min |
| 09:30 | Returns/RMA analysis | Ops | 30 min |
| 10:00 | Quality issues triage | QC | 30 min |
| 14:00 | Customer feedback review | CS | 30 min |

**Questions to answer:**
- What complaints came in last week?
- Are there patterns in returns?
- What quality issues were flagged?
- What's the customer sentiment trend?

### Wednesday: Commercial Review

| Time | Activity | Owner | Duration |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 | Sales performance review | Sales Lead | 30 min |
| 10:00 | Margin analysis | Finance | 30 min |
| 11:00 | Competitor activity check | Marketing | 30 min |
| 14:00 | Pricing review (if needed) | Commercial | 30 min |

**Key metrics to review:**
- Revenue vs target (by channel)
- New accounts opened
- Margin by category
- Competitor price movements

### Thursday: Inventory & Supply Chain

| Time | Activity | Owner | Duration |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 | Stock level review | Buying | 45 min |
| 10:00 | Supplier performance | Buying | 30 min |
| 11:00 | Inbound shipment tracking | Warehouse | 30 min |
| 14:00 | Slow mover review | Commercial | 30 min |

**Inventory checkpoints:**
- [ ] Days of stock by A/B/C category
- [ ] Stockouts (current and forecast)
- [ ] Overstock (>90 days)
- [ ] Supplier lead times vs actuals
- [ ] Inbound delays

### Friday: Reporting & Reflection

| Time | Activity | Owner | Duration |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| 09:00 | Weekly report compilation | Ops | 1 hour |
| 11:00 | Leadership review | Directors | 30 min |
| 14:00 | Team wins/learnings | All | 15 min |
| 15:00 | Next week prep | Ops Lead | 30 min |

## Daily Standups (15 min max)

**Time:** [CUSTOMIZE: e.g., 08:45 daily]  
**Format:** In-person or video, standing

**Each team member covers:**
1. What I completed yesterday
2. What I'm working on today
3. What's blocking me

**Rules:**
- Start on time, end on time
- No problem-solving in standup (take offline)
- Blockers get assigned an owner immediately

## Escalation Framework

### When to Escalate

| Issue | Escalation Level | Timeline |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| Stockout on A-line | Ops Manager | Same day |
| Customer complaint (serious) | CS Manager | 4 hours |
| Supplier delay (major) | Buying Manager | Same day |
| System outage | IT + Ops Manager | Immediately |
| Quality defect (batch) | QC + Ops Manager | 2 hours |
| Safety incident | Director | Immediately |
| [CUSTOMIZE: Your escalations] | | |

### Escalation Template

```
ESCALATION: [Brief title]

SITUATION: What happened?
IMPACT: Who/what is affected? (orders, revenue, customers)
TIMELINE: When did it start? When do we need resolution?
OPTIONS: What can we do?
RECOMMENDATION: What should we do?
DECISION NEEDED BY: [Name] by [Time]
```
