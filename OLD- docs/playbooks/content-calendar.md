# Content Calendar Playbook

Monthly content planning for consistent, strategic marketing.

## Overview

This playbook guides monthly content planning across all channels. Consistent, strategic content builds authority, drives organic traffic, and nurtures leads through the funnel.

**Goal**: Plan and schedule one month of content that aligns with business objectives and audience needs.

**Timeline**: Complete by 25th of preceding month (e.g., February content planned by January 25th).

**Frequency**: Monthly

**Owner**: Marketing Manager / Content Lead

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Access to analytics (Google Analytics, social insights)
- [ ] Previous month's content performance data
- [ ] Business calendar (product launches, sales, events)
- [ ] Content management system access
- [ ] Social media scheduling tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, etc.)
- [ ] Content calendar template (Google Sheets or similar)

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Review Previous Month Performance (Day 1-2)

**Learn from what worked and what didn't.**

1. **Website Content (Blog)**
   - Top 5 posts by pageviews
   - Top 5 posts by time on page
   - Posts with highest conversion (email signup, purchase)
   - Underperformers: High traffic, high bounce

2. **Social Media**
   - Top 3 posts per platform by engagement
   - Follower growth/decline
   - Best posting times (when did engagement peak?)
   - Content types that performed (video vs. image vs. text)

3. **Email**
   - Open rates by campaign
   - Click-through rates
   - Best subject lines
   - Unsubscribe patterns

4. **Document Insights**
   - What topics resonated?
   - What formats worked?
   - What timing was optimal?
   - What to replicate/avoid?

### Step 2: Identify Themes & Topics (Day 2-3)

**Decide what to talk about.**

1. **Business Priorities**
   - Product launches this month?
   - Sales or promotions?
   - Industry events or trade shows?
   - Seasonal relevance?

2. **Audience Needs**
   - Common customer questions (check support tickets)
   - Trending industry topics
   - Competitor content gaps
   - Keyword opportunities (from SEO research)

3. **Content Pillars**
   
   | Pillar | Description | Example Topics |
   |--------|-------------|----------------|
   | Product Education | How to use our products | Installation guides, tips |
   | Industry Expertise | Position as thought leader | Trends, best practices |
   | Customer Success | Social proof | Case studies, testimonials |
   | Behind the Scenes | Build brand connection | Team, process, values |

4. **Theme Selection**
   - Choose 2-3 themes for the month
   - Ensure variety across pillars
   - Align with business calendar

### Step 3: Map Content to Funnel Stages (Day 3-4)

**Different content for different buying stages.**

1. **Top of Funnel (Awareness)**
   - Goal: Attract new audience
   - Content: Blog posts, social media, videos
   - Topics: Industry trends, how-to guides, problem awareness
   - Example: "5 Signs You're Using the Wrong Cable Ties"

2. **Middle of Funnel (Consideration)**
   - Goal: Educate and build trust
   - Content: Comparison guides, case studies, webinars
   - Topics: Product comparisons, use cases, expert tips
   - Example: "Industrial vs. Standard Cable Ties: Which Do You Need?"

3. **Bottom of Funnel (Decision)**
   - Goal: Convert to customer
   - Content: Product demos, testimonials, special offers
   - Topics: Product features, customer results, buying guides
   - Example: "How GTSE Cable Ties Saved Company X 20% on Installation Time"

4. **Balance Check**
   - Aim for: 50% TOFU, 30% MOFU, 20% BOFU
   - Adjust based on business goals (launches = more BOFU)

### Step 4: Assign Content Types (Day 4-5)

**Match topics to the right format and channel.**

1. **Content Type Matrix**
   
   | Type | Effort | Channels | Best For |
   |------|--------|----------|----------|
   | Blog Post | Medium | Website, Email, LinkedIn | SEO, education |
   | Social Post | Low | All social | Engagement, awareness |
   | Video | High | YouTube, Social, Website | Demos, tutorials |
   | Email | Medium | Email list | Nurturing, announcements |
   | Infographic | Medium | Social, Blog | Data, comparisons |
   | Case Study | High | Website, Sales | BOFU conversion |

2. **Channel Strategy**
   
   | Channel | Frequency | Primary Content |
   |---------|-----------|-----------------|
   | Blog | 2-4 posts/month | Long-form, SEO |
   | LinkedIn | 3-5x/week | Industry, B2B |
   | Instagram | 3-5x/week | Visual, lifestyle |
   | Email | 2-4x/month | Curated, offers |
   | YouTube | 1-2x/month | How-to, demos |

3. **Assign Types to Topics**
   - Each theme gets multiple content pieces
   - Repurpose: 1 blog → 3-5 social posts + 1 email
   - Mix formats for variety

### Step 5: Create Content Briefs (Day 5-7)

**Give creators clear direction.**

1. **Blog Post Brief**
   ```
   Title: [Working title]
   Keyword: [Primary SEO keyword]
   Word Count: [Target length]
   Audience: [Who is this for?]
   Funnel Stage: [TOFU/MOFU/BOFU]
   Objective: [What should reader do after?]
   Outline:
   - H2: [Section 1]
   - H2: [Section 2]
   - H2: [Section 3]
   CTA: [Call to action]
   Internal Links: [Link to these pages]
   Deadline: [Date]
   Owner: [Who's writing]
   ```

2. **Social Post Brief**
   ```
   Platform: [Instagram/LinkedIn/etc.]
   Hook: [First line to grab attention]
   Body: [Key message]
   CTA: [What should they do?]
   Visual: [Image/video description]
   Hashtags: [Relevant tags]
   Post Date: [Date and time]
   Owner: [Who's creating]
   ```

3. **Email Brief**
   ```
   Campaign: [Name]
   Subject Line Options: [2-3 options]
   Preview Text: [First line]
   Objective: [Goal]
   Segment: [Who receives]
   Body: [Key sections]
   CTA: [Primary action]
   Send Date: [Date]
   Owner: [Who's writing]
   ```

### Step 6: Schedule Production (Day 7-10)

**Assign deadlines and owners.**

1. **Production Timeline**
   
   | Milestone | Days Before Publish |
   |-----------|---------------------|
   | Brief approved | 14 days |
   | First draft | 10 days |
   | Review/edits | 7 days |
   | Final approval | 3 days |
   | Scheduled | 1 day |
   | Published | 0 |

2. **Assign Owners**
   - Writer/Creator
   - Editor/Reviewer
   - Designer (if visual needed)
   - Approver

3. **Add to Project Management**
   - Create tasks with deadlines
   - Set up review workflows
   - Link to briefs and assets

### Step 7: Distribution Plan (Day 10-12)

**Content without distribution is wasted effort.**

1. **Publishing Schedule**
   - Blog: Tuesday/Thursday mornings
   - LinkedIn: Weekdays 8-10am
   - Instagram: Weekdays 12pm, 6pm
   - Email: Tuesday or Thursday 10am

2. **Repurposing Workflow**
   ```
   Blog Post Published
   ↓
   Day 0: Share on LinkedIn (full summary)
   Day 1: Share on Twitter (thread)
   Day 2: Instagram carousel (key points)
   Day 7: Email newsletter feature
   Day 14: Repost on LinkedIn (new angle)
   Day 30: Pinterest (if visual)
   ```

3. **Promotion Checklist**
   - [ ] Organic social posts scheduled
   - [ ] Email to subscribers (if relevant)
   - [ ] Internal team notified to engage
   - [ ] Paid promotion planned (if budget)
   - [ ] Influencer/partner shares (if applicable)

---

## Checklist

Before the month begins:

- [ ] Previous month performance reviewed and documented
- [ ] 2-3 themes identified and approved
- [ ] Content mapped to funnel stages (50/30/20)
- [ ] Content types assigned to each piece
- [ ] Briefs created for all content
- [ ] Production deadlines set
- [ ] Owners assigned
- [ ] Distribution schedule set
- [ ] Calendar shared with team
