# Competitor Deep Dive Playbook

Full competitive analysis to inform strategy and identify opportunities.

## Overview

Know your competition better than they know themselves. This deep dive provides actionable intelligence on competitors' strengths, weaknesses, and strategies to inform GTSE's positioning and tactics.

**Goal**: Complete analysis of top 5 competitors with actionable recommendations.

**Timeline**: 2-3 weeks for thorough analysis.

**Frequency**: Quarterly (with monthly spot-checks on key competitors)

**Owner**: Strategy / Marketing

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Competitor list (top 5 identified)
- [ ] Access to competitor websites (no login required)
- [ ] Amazon Seller Central access
- [ ] Helium 10 or Jungle Scout (for Amazon data)
- [ ] SimilarWeb or SEMrush (for web traffic data)
- [ ] Social media access (public viewing)
- [ ] Analysis template spreadsheet

---

## Steps

### Step 1: Identify Top 5 Competitors (Day 1)

**Know who you're up against.**

1. **Direct Competitors**
   - Sell same/similar products
   - Target same customer segments
   - Compete on same channels (Amazon, web, trade)

2. **Indirect Competitors**
   - Substitute products
   - Adjacent categories
   - Different price points but same need

3. **Selection Criteria**
   - Market share / size
   - Visibility (who shows up in searches?)
   - Customer mentions (who do customers compare us to?)
   - Growth trajectory (rising threats)

4. **Document Competitor Profiles**
   
   | Competitor | Type | HQ | Est. Size | Key Channels |
   |------------|------|-----|-----------|--------------|
   | Competitor A | Direct | UK | £5-10M | Amazon, Web |
   | Competitor B | Direct | China | £20M+ | Amazon, Trade |
   | Competitor C | Indirect | UK | £2-5M | Web, Trade |
   | ... | | | | |

### Step 2: Website Analysis (Days 2-5)

**Dissect their online presence.**

1. **UX & Design Audit**
   - First impression (professional, dated, modern?)
   - Navigation clarity
   - Mobile experience
   - Page load speed (use GTmetrix)
   - Search functionality
   - Checkout process (if visible)

2. **Messaging Analysis**
   - Value proposition (what's their headline promise?)
   - Unique selling points
   - Brand voice and tone
   - Trust signals (reviews, certifications, guarantees)
   - Pain points addressed

3. **Pricing Strategy**
   - Price comparison on key products
   - Volume/trade pricing visible?
   - Discount strategies
   - Free shipping thresholds
   - Price positioning (premium, value, budget)

4. **Product Catalog**
   - Range breadth (how many SKUs?)
   - Categories covered
   - Private label vs. branded
   - New product cadence
   - Out of stock patterns

5. **Technical SEO**
   - Domain authority (use Moz or Ahrefs)
   - Top ranking keywords
   - Organic traffic estimate (SimilarWeb)
   - Backlink profile
   - Site structure

**Document Findings:**
```

Website: [URL]

UX Score: [1-10]
Messaging Score: [1-10]
Price Position: [Premium/Value/Budget]
Estimated SKUs: [Number]

Monthly Traffic: [Estimate]

Strengths:
-
-

Weaknesses:
-
-
```

### Step 3: Amazon Analysis (Days 5-8)

**Where most battles are won or lost.**

1. **Listing Quality**
   - Title optimization
   - Bullet point effectiveness
   - Image quality and quantity
   - A+ Content presence/quality
   - Review count and rating

2. **Performance Metrics** (via Helium 10/Jungle Scout)
   - Estimated monthly revenue
   - BSR (Best Seller Rank) trends
   - Keyword rankings
   - Review velocity

3. **Pricing & Promotions**
   - Current pricing vs. GTSE
   - Coupon usage
   - Lightning deals frequency
   - Subscribe & Save
   - Bundle offers

4. **Review Intelligence**
   - Common positive themes
   - Common complaints
   - Response rate to reviews
   - Quality issues mentioned
   - Opportunities from unmet needs

5. **Advertising Presence**
   - Sponsored Products presence
   - Sponsored Brands
   - Sponsored Display
   - Video ads

### Step 4: Content Audit (Days 8-11)

**Content = authority. Who's winning?**

1. **Blog/Resource Center**
   - Publishing frequency
   - Content depth and quality
   - Topics covered
   - SEO optimization
   - Engagement (comments, shares)

2. **Social Media Presence**
   
   | Platform | Followers | Post Freq. | Engagement | Content Type |
   |----------|-----------|------------|------------|--------------|
   | LinkedIn | | | | |
   | Instagram | | | | |
   | Facebook | | | | |
   | YouTube | | | | |
   | TikTok | | | | |

3. **Email Marketing**
   - Sign up for competitor emails
   - Frequency of sends
   - Types of content (promotional, educational, news)
   - Design quality
   - Personalization level

4. **Video Content**
   - YouTube presence
   - Product demos
   - How-to content
   - Brand videos
   - Production quality

5. **Downloadable Resources**
   - Catalogs, spec sheets
   - Guides, whitepapers
   - Tools, calculators
   - Gated vs. ungated

### Step 5: SWOT for Each Competitor (Days 11-13)

**Structured analysis for strategic insight.**

For each competitor, complete:

```
Competitor: [Name]

STRENGTHS
(What do they do better than us?)
- 
-
-

WEAKNESSES
(Where do they fall short?)
-
-
-

OPPORTUNITIES
(External factors we could exploit)
-
-
-

THREATS
(External factors that could help them)
-
-
-
```

**Guiding Questions:**
- Strengths: Why do customers choose them? What do they do well?
- Weaknesses: What complaints do their customers have? What are they missing?
- Opportunities: Market gaps they're not filling? Trends they're ignoring?
- Threats: Are they innovating? Growing? Raising investment?

### Step 6: Opportunities for GTSE (Days 13-15)

**Turn intelligence into action.**

1. **Competitive Gaps**
   - Products they don't offer that we do/could
   - Customer segments they're ignoring
   - Channels they're weak on
   - Content topics they're missing

2. **Positioning Opportunities**
   - Claims we can make that they can't
   - Unique differentiators to emphasize
   - Price/value positioning options
   - Service advantages

3. **Quick Wins**
   - Improvements we can make immediately
   - Low-effort, high-impact changes
   - Messaging tweaks
   - Listing optimizations

4. **Strategic Initiatives**
   - Longer-term opportunities
   - New product development ideas
   - Channel expansion
   - Partnership opportunities

### Step 7: Action Recommendations (Days 15-17)

**Prioritized recommendations with owners and timelines.**

1. **Recommendation Format**
   ```
   Recommendation: [What to do]
   Rationale: [Why it matters]
   Competitor Insight: [What prompted this]
   Priority: [High/Medium/Low]
   Effort: [Low/Medium/High]
   Owner: [Who]
   Timeline: [When]
   Expected Impact: [What we'll gain]
   ```

2. **Prioritization Matrix**
   
   |  | Low Effort | High Effort |
   |--|------------|-------------|
   | **High Impact** | DO NOW | PLAN |
   | **Low Impact** | MAYBE | DON'T |

3. **Example Recommendations**
   - "Update Amazon titles to match winning competitor format (High priority, Low effort)"
   - "Develop 5 new comparison blog posts targeting competitor brand + category keywords (Medium priority, Medium effort)"
   - "Launch YouTube how-to series to counter Competitor B's video presence (Medium priority, High effort)"

4. **Summary Report**
   - Executive summary (1 page)
   - Key findings (top 5 insights)
   - Prioritized recommendations (top 10)
   - Detailed competitor profiles (appendix)
