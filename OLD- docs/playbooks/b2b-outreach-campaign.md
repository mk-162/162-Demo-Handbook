# B2B Outreach Campaign Playbook

## Overview

Systematic process for cold outreach to target B2B accounts. This playbook covers identifying ideal customers, multi-channel engagement (email, LinkedIn, phone), and moving prospects from cold contact to booked meeting. Typical campaign duration: 4-6 weeks.

**Use this when:**
- Expanding into new market segments
- Launching B2B-focused products
- Building pipeline for trade accounts
- Re-engaging dormant B2B prospects

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) defined
- [ ] CRM access configured (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)
- [ ] Email domain warmed up (if new)
- [ ] LinkedIn Sales Navigator access (recommended)
- [ ] Value proposition refined for target segment
- [ ] Case studies/references available

---

## Phase 1: Target Identification (Days 1-3)

### Step 1: Define Target Criteria
**What**: Establish specific parameters for ideal accounts

**How**:
1. Industry/vertical (e.g., construction, manufacturing)
2. Company size (employee count, revenue range)
3. Geography (UK, specific regions)
4. Technology indicators (tools they use)
5. Buying signals (hiring, expansion, funding)

**Output**: Documented ICP criteria for this campaign

**Tools**: CRM, past customer analysis

---

### Step 2: Build Target Account List
**What**: Compile list of 100-200 target companies

**How**:
1. Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator Account Search
2. Filter by industry, size, location
3. Cross-reference with existing CRM (avoid duplicates)
4. Verify companies are legitimate and active
5. Prioritize accounts (Tier 1, 2, 3)

**Output**: Target account list with 100-200 companies

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo.io, ZoomInfo

---

### Step 3: Identify Decision Makers
**What**: Find 2-3 contacts per target account

**How**:
1. Search for relevant job titles:
   - Purchasing Manager/Director
   - Operations Manager
   - Facilities Manager
   - Business Owner
2. Capture: Name, Title, Email, LinkedIn URL, Phone
3. Verify email addresses
4. Note any mutual connections

**Output**: Contact database with 200-600 contacts

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Hunter.io, Clearbit

---

## Phase 2: Research & Personalization (Days 4-7)

### Step 4: Account Research
**What**: Gather intelligence on top-tier accounts

**How**:
1. Review company website (products, news, about page)
2. Check recent press releases/announcements
3. Review LinkedIn company page for updates
4. Identify pain points based on industry
5. Find personalization hooks (recent hires, awards, events)

**Output**: Research notes for Tier 1 accounts (top 20-30)

**Tools**: Company websites, LinkedIn, Google News

---

### Step 5: Create Email Templates
**What**: Develop email sequence with 5 touchpoints

**How**:
1. **Email 1 (Day 1)**: Introduction + value prop
2. **Email 2 (Day 4)**: Social proof/case study
3. **Email 3 (Day 8)**: Problem agitation + solution
4. **Email 4 (Day 14)**: Different angle/offer
5. **Email 5 (Day 21)**: Breakup email

**Template Structure**:
- Subject: Short, curiosity-driven
- Opening: Personalized hook
- Body: One key value point
- CTA: Clear, low-friction ask

**Output**: 5-email sequence with A/B test variants

**Tools**: Email platform, copywriting templates

---

### Step 6: Prepare LinkedIn Content
**What**: Create LinkedIn connection request and message templates

**How**:
1. Connection request (under 300 chars): No pitch, just connect
2. Welcome message: Thank for connecting, share value
3. Follow-up messages: Aligned with email sequence
4. Content to share: Relevant articles, insights

**Output**: LinkedIn message sequence (3-4 templates)

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator

---

## Phase 3: Email Campaign Execution (Days 8-28)

### Step 7: Load Contacts into Sequence
**What**: Upload contacts and launch email campaign

**How**:
1. Import contacts to email platform
2. Segment by tier/priority
3. Apply appropriate sequence
4. Set sending schedule (9-11 AM, Tue-Thu optimal)
5. Limit daily sends (50-100 for warm domain)
6. Enable open/click tracking

**Output**: Active email sequences with scheduled sends

**Tools**: Outreach.io, Lemlist, HubSpot Sequences, Apollo

---

### Step 8: Monitor and Respond
**What**: Track engagement and respond to replies

**How**:
1. Check for replies twice daily
2. Respond to interested replies within 2 hours
3. Handle objections with prepared responses
4. Remove bounces and unsubscribes immediately
5. Track metrics:
   - Open rate (target: 40%+)
   - Reply rate (target: 5-10%)
   - Meeting book rate (target: 2-5%)

**Output**: Daily activity log, qualified opportunities

**Tools**: Email platform, CRM

---

### Step 9: A/B Test and Optimize
**What**: Improve performance through testing

**How**:
1. Test subject lines (Week 1)
2. Test opening lines (Week 2)
3. Test CTAs (Week 3)
4. Promote winning variants
5. Adjust send times based on open data

**Output**: Optimized email sequence

**Tools**: Email platform analytics

---

## Phase 4: LinkedIn Engagement (Parallel to Email)

### Step 10: Send Connection Requests
**What**: Connect with decision makers on LinkedIn

**How**:
1. Send 20-30 connection requests per day
2. Use personalized note (mutual connection, shared interest)
3. Do NOT pitch in connection request
4. Accept all incoming connections
5. Track acceptance rate (target: 30%+)

**Output**: Growing network of target contacts

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator

---

### Step 11: Engage with Content
**What**: Build familiarity before messaging

**How**:
1. Like and comment on prospect's posts
2. Share relevant content they might find valuable
3. Engage authentically (no generic "Great post!")
4. Note engagement for personalization
5. Spend 15-20 min daily on social selling

**Output**: Warmer prospects, engagement history

**Tools**: LinkedIn

---

### Step 12: LinkedIn Message Sequence
**What**: Send direct messages to connected prospects

**How**:
1. Wait 2-3 days after connection acceptance
2. Send value-first message (no pitch)
3. Follow up with insight/content share
4. Third message: Soft meeting ask
5. Coordinate with email (don't double-hit same day)

**Output**: LinkedIn conversations, meeting requests

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator

---

## Phase 5: Phone Follow-Up (Days 21-35)

### Step 13: Prioritize Call List
**What**: Identify highest-priority phone outreach targets

**How**:
1. Sort by engagement (email opens, clicks, replies)
2. Prioritize Tier 1 accounts
3. Focus on contacts who showed interest but didn't book
4. Verify phone numbers before calling
5. Block 2-hour calling windows

**Output**: Prioritized call list (50-100 contacts)

**Tools**: CRM, phone number verification

---

### Step 14: Execute Call Campaign
**What**: Make phone calls to warm prospects

**How**:
1. Prepare call script with key talking points
2. Reference previous engagement ("I sent an email about...")
3. Have answer to common objections ready
4. Goal: Book meeting or qualify out
5. Leave voicemail only on 2nd attempt
6. Log all calls in CRM immediately

**Call Script Framework**:
- Introduction (10 sec)
- Reason for call (15 sec)
- Value statement (20 sec)
- Question to engage (open-ended)
- Meeting ask or next step

**Output**: Completed call log, booked meetings

**Tools**: Phone, CRM, call tracking

---

### Step 15: Voicemail Strategy
**What**: Leave effective voicemails when needed

**How**:
1. Keep under 30 seconds
2. State name, company, and one value point
3. Reference email sent
4. Don't ask them to call back (reduces friction)
5. Say you'll try again or send info via email

**Output**: Voicemails that complement email sequence

**Tools**: Phone

---

## Phase 6: CRM Tracking & Meeting Booking (Ongoing)

### Step 16: Maintain CRM Hygiene
**What**: Keep accurate records of all activity

**How**:
1. Log every touchpoint (email, call, LinkedIn)
2. Update contact status:
   - New > Contacted > Engaged > Opportunity > Won/Lost
3. Add notes on conversations
4. Set follow-up tasks
5. Tag for campaign tracking

**Output**: Clean, actionable CRM data

**Tools**: HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive

---

### Step 17: Book and Confirm Meetings
**What**: Convert interest into scheduled meetings

**How**:
1. Use calendar booking link (Calendly, HubSpot)
2. Offer 2-3 specific time options
3. Send calendar invite with:
   - Clear agenda
   - Dial-in/video link
   - Brief about us
4. Send confirmation email
5. Reminder 24 hours before
6. Reminder 1 hour before

**Output**: Confirmed meetings on calendar

**Tools**: Calendly, Teams/Zoom, CRM

---

### Step 18: Handoff to Sales
**What**: Transition qualified meetings to sales team

**How**:
1. Complete meeting prep notes:
   - Company background
   - Contact info and role
   - Pain points identified
   - Previous engagement history
   - Suggested talking points
2. Brief sales rep before meeting
3. Update CRM with opportunity details
4. Track outcome and feedback

**Output**: Sales-ready opportunity with context

**Tools**: CRM, internal handoff document
